The journey

One bag, two hillsides.

Every bag of Kinini makes the same journey — from a terraced hillside in the Northern Province, through a woman-owned washing station, into a roastery on the English south coast. Nothing about that story is invented for marketing; it's simply how the coffee gets to you.

01

Grown on the hillside

Mature trees — some planted over a decade ago — are tended, pruned and organically treated by farmers across the Rusiga and Mbogo sectors, with on-the-job agronomy support to keep quality consistent harvest to harvest.

02

Washed & hand-graded

Cherries are processed at the Kinini washing station and hand-sorted, bowl by bowl, by the cooperative's women — the same careful grading that earns neighbouring lots Cup of Excellence recognition.

03

Roasted in Eastbourne

Green beans are shipped directly and roasted in small batches by Jacquie herself, by hand, to order — so what lands on your doorstep was roasted days, not months, before it arrives.

“We built Kinini so the women growing this coffee keep ownership of it, from the hillside to the cup.”
