The journey

Three hands, one bag.

From hillside to roastery, every step is done by people who own their part of the story.

GROWN

Rulindo hillside

620+ farmers across 7 cooperatives tend trees on terraces 1,800–2,500m up, some planted 30 years ago.

GRADED

Washing station

Cherries washed and hand-sorted by the cooperative's women — bowl by bowl, on-site.

ROASTED

Eastbourne roastery

Jacquie roasts every batch by hand and ships within 48 hours — fresh, not warehoused.

A cooperative, not a charity.

Kinini's farmers own their crop end to end — Kinini exists to help them keep it that way.
